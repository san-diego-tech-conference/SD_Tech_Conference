The San Diego Undergraduate Tech Conference is a student-run program and event designed to celebrate the creativity and innovation of undergraduate work in computing and data sciences.

It is a space where students can connect to resources and structure to develop passion projects, establish a startup, and dive deeper into research.

The conference event gives undergrads experience practicing technical public speaking and networking, while papers provide publication opportunities.

Our Mission

  • Empowering Undergraduates – Providing a structured and accessible platform for students to develop, present, and discuss their work.
  • Encouraging Collaboration – Fostering a dynamic community of thinkers, builders, and innovators.
  • Ensuring Fair Recognition – Through a blind review process with experienced UCSD TAs, lecturers, and alumni, every submission is evaluated on its merit.

Collaborators

The Basement at UCSD

The Basement at UC San Diego is an innovation and entrepreneurship hub open to students from all disciplines who want to explore ideas and develop them into impactful ventures. The Basement provides mentorship, connections with serial entrepreneurs and industry experts, funding opportunities, hands-on workshops, and collaborative spaces that support every stage of the entrepreneurial journey. Through its two flagship programs, The Basement fosters creativity, community, and a spirit of innovation:

  • Blackstone LaunchPad supports students actively building early-stage ventures by providing mentorship, guidance, and resources to accelerate their entrepreneurial journey.
  • Innovating for X (i4X) brings together multidisciplinary teams to tackle real-world challenges from corporate, government, and nonprofit partners— gaining workforce-ready experience while developing disruptive solutions.
